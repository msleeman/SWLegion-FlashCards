## Thrawn + Imperial Agent Combo (Deep Dive)

- **27:29** — Thrawn mirrors the Dooku/Super Attack token-sharing loop but is dramatically better. Setup: Thrawn has **Tactical** (free aim on move) + **Strategize 2**. He moves (picks up aim), then Strategizes 2 to give aim+dodge to self and aim+dodge to the Imperial Agent.
- **28:39** — Agent has the **Target** keyword (potential extra aim) and also has **Tactical + Relentless**, meaning he can move twice and funnel even more aims back to Thrawn via Exemplar.
- **29:48** — Thrawn has **Courage 3** (vs. Dooku's Courage 2), making Compel much easier. Net result: **3–5 aim tokens + 2 dodge tokens** between the two units before spending anything on attacks.
- **32:23** — Baseline Thrawn/Agent is already "Dooku-esque combo turned up to 11" — before any command cards or tricks.
- **32:40** — Thrawn in melee: 4 red dice, **Lethal 1** (functions as Duelist via aims), native dodge tokens, **Immune Pierce**. Called "the best 5-health red commander."

### Thrawn's Command Cards

- **33:27** — **One-pip (Cunning):** Orders Thrawn + any other Imperial unit within Range 2 of an enemy. Creates "damned if you do, damned if you don't" board states (e.g., forces opponent's Yoda out of melee into open fire, or stay in and get beaten). Because it's cunning and Implacable has Command & Control Uplink, Thrawn wins any 0v0 priority tie.
- **34:24** — **Two-pip:** After an enemy unit activates, you can move or attack with *any* non-suppressed, non-panicked friendly unit within Range 2 of that enemy. Very few restrictions — not a true standby but sometimes better. Main use: double-tapping melee targets or attacking units the opponent thought were safe.
- **41:28** — **Rules question:** If Thrawn is dead, can you still discard his two-pip? Hosts say probably yes RAW (you're not nominating him as commander, just discarding a card for an effect), but a forum ruling might say no.

## Tarkin Discussion

- **38:00** — **Cheating correction:** You *cannot* use Pull the Strings to give Thrawn a second Strategize — Pull the Strings grants an **attack action**, not a free activation.
- **38:16** — Tarkin's **Pull the Strings** value: it's an extra attack *exactly when you need it* from *exactly the unit you need*. Flexibility is the key — can also be used as a move in a pinch.
- **47:03** — With Thrawn + Tarkin + Agent, you have 3 sources of Direct/Entourage: Tarkin (entourage Vader), Thrawn (direct any Imperial), Agent (direct core unit). Baseline **3 Demoralize tokens per turn** even on Standing Orders, potentially 5+.

## Han Solo (90 pts) Analysis

- **1:04:25** — Han at 90 points considered insane value for Rebels. Replacing regular Han is an automatic upgrade.
- **1:05:05** — Thrawn/Tarkin considered a bigger meta shift than Han because it's a list-wide strategy plus new command cards, while Han is more of a unit upgrade.
- **1:08:36** — Han: **Speed 3, Unstoppable without Plotting**. Plays on a wing, constantly reducing the dice pool that can reach him.
- **1:09:46** — Han's weaknesses: struggles against Thrawn (two-pip can hit him after he charges in), hates standbys, white defense dice with no cover means concentrated fire can kill him quickly.
- **1:11:19** — Super Shore squad trap: with multiple aims from Thrawn, a super shore shot could one-shot Han; even with Exemplar sharing (~2 aims on shores), Han likely survives at ~1–2 wounds.
- **1:13:27** — Thrawn two-pip counter: after Han charges in and kills shore models, discard Thrawn two-pip to swing back at Han immediately.
- **1:14:05** — Priority mind game: Han player likely opens with Change of Plans to bait Thrawn one-pip. If Thrawn player is wise, they play a two-pip instead and force the Han player to adapt.

## Mace Windu Assessment

- **1:17:22** — **165 pts.** Vapad mechanic: gets stronger as he takes wounds, but relies on getting hurt early without dying.
- **1:19:36** — **Compel Core Trooper** (NOT Clone Trooper) — does NOT work on Arcs or Commandos.
- **1:21:36** — Only **one personal command card** (Vapad: adds/upgrades dice, allows self-crit for extra dice). Shared Jedi Council command cards also require jumping through multiple hoops for token payoffs.
- **1:22:05** — Doesn't change board state like Anakin's triple-move/double-attack card or Thrawn's two-pip. As a beatstick compared to Anakin (similar cost), Anakin does more damage via Tempted + command cards and a better command hand.
- **1:24:33** — Question raised: why did players stop running Anakin? Probably shifted to cheap, fast objective units. Anakin + naked ARCs combo hasn't been fully explored and could still be strong.

## GAR/Republic Faction Issues

- **1:26:48** — Republic command cards *generally* don't change board state. Cody 3-pip requires multiple setup steps. Even promising new units like Shock Teth appear to have only one impactful command card.
- **1:27:22** — With expensive characters (110–165 pts each), Republic can only fit 1–2 in a list and needs interesting command cards from *both* to have a healthy hand. The new units don't provide that.
- **1:31:01** — Even current lists with Yoda are forced to run generic cards like Ambush, Push, and Assault to fill the command hand.
- **1:35:17** — GAR is the **only faction without a "pitch command card for effect" mechanic**: Empire has Thrawn, Rebels have Grogu and Lando, CIS has Asajj/Dooku 3-pip.
- **1:36:50** — Hope pinned on **Bokeatan + Axe Woves** (releasing June) to inject command card depth via Clan Cre Mando support.
- **1:37:25** — Prediction: In a year, competitive GAR will be Yoda + Clan Cre Mandos, using Mando command cards to fill the hand instead of Republic-specific cards.
- **1:38:02** — Notable: Grogu has the same number of command cards as Mace Windu. Grogu is a support token, not even a unit.
- **1:32:14** — Suggested fix: Drop Wookie Chieftain 35 pts and all three Wookie command cards become playable — would inject life into the faction. Hosts agree this is exactly why it won't happen.
